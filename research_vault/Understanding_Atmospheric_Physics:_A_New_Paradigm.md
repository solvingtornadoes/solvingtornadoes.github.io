Short Answer Quiz: Answer Key
* Meteorologists often assume moist air is lighter than dry air based solely on the lower molecular weight of water. However, this ignores the fact that H₂O molecules do not exist in isolation in the atmosphere but form clusters, negating the simple weight comparison.
* H₂O's ability to neutralize its own polarity through hydrogen bonding explains its fluidity and low viscosity. This variable polarity is key to understanding water's behavior in various states and its role in atmospheric phenomena.
* The "atmospheric plumbing" model views the atmosphere as a system of flow driven by vortices acting as pipes, channeling energy and moisture. This contrasts with the "atmospheric engine" metaphor, which lacks a defined mechanism for driving atmospheric processes.
* The simple label of water as a polar molecule fails to account for its fluidity and other anomalies. It neglects the dynamic nature of water's polarity, which is influenced by hydrogen bonding and varies depending on the molecule's environment.
* At the surface of water, some hydrogen bonds are disrupted, leading to a partial restoration of polarity in the surface molecules. This increased attraction between surface molecules creates the phenomenon of surface tension.
* Wind shear, the difference in wind speed or direction at different altitudes, creates the conditions for spinning microdroplets of water. This spinning amplifies surface tension, leading to the formation of structured vortices.
* Vortices, acting as "atmospheric pipes," can channel low-pressure energy from the jet stream down to ground level. This delivery of low pressure is a key factor in the formation and intensification of storms.
* The "plasma" stage refers to the rapid spinning of H₂O molecules in vortices, forming polymer-like chains with increased structural integrity. This plasma-like state allows vortices to maintain their shape and energy over long distances.
* The proposed strategy involves licensing the new storm mitigation technology to companies in various industries, promoting rapid adoption while retaining control over core intellectual property. This ensures wider benefits and faster implementation of the technology.
* Recognizing that H₂O's polarity is not static but changes based on hydrogen bonding is crucial for understanding its diverse behaviors. Accurately modeling atmospheric phenomena depends on incorporating this variable polarity into our understanding of water's role in atmospheric processes.
