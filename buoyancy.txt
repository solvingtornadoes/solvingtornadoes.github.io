Title: The Failure of Buoyancy and the Reality of Scale
Core Thesis: The conventional meteorological reliance on buoyancy is fundamentally flawed. It fails to account for the physics of scale and the actual weight of atmospheric water.
Observable Evidence: Anyone can look up at a cloud and surmise that the droplets suspended within it are large and, therefore, very heavy compared to the surrounding drier air. Despite this distinct difference in weight, we can plainly see that clouds do not routinely drop out of the sky.
The Failure of Standard Theory: Standard meteorology refuses to address this contradiction. The establishment sticks to the buoyancy argument not because it is physically sound, but because people are lazy-minded and stubborn, showing little interest in exploring the actual mechanics keeping that water suspended.
