The Debye shielding effect is a feature of all plasmas, where the physical dimensions of the system are larger than the Debye length (λD). The Debye length measures the distance at which the effect of the electric field of an individual charged particle is felt by other charged particles inside the plasma. The shielding occurs due to high plasma conductivity, allowing electrical current to flow freely and short out interior electric fields. Debye shielding is not dominated by collisions or an external field
